How To Play Hits and Blows

In this game, the computer picks a sequence of four colors out of a list of six, with no repeats. For example, it might generate blue, red, orange, pink, but never pink, pink, green, green. Your goal as the player is to guess this sequence in 8 attempts or less.

Each turn, you make your guess by clicking a color on the right in order to select it. Then click on the white square you believe will be that color. Remember, there will be no duplicates.

Once you've added a color to all four squares, click "Check" at the bottom of the column to see if you were right. The computer will tell you how close you were in the 2x2 grid above each column.

A red square is a hit. This means that one of your squares has the correct color AND the correct position. A yellow square is a blow, which means one of the colors is correct but NOT in the right position. A blank square is a miss, meaning that neither the color or position was correct.

Keep in mind that the order of the hits and blows does NOT correspond to the sequence of your guesses. A hit means that ANY of the squares is a hit, etc.
